Transform

Transform helps us make changes to the content we decide to transform.

It is important to know that if we have various elements in a parent element, and we transform that parent, all the children will also be changed.

Also, any elements inside a div, paragraph or the element we transform will be affected, be a button or anything else.

ORIGINAL REFERENCE
transform: rotate(10deg);

This transform rotates our div by 10 degrees.

transform: scale(1.2);

This transform makes our div 1.2 times as big as originally, it can be 0.5 to make it half of the original size.

transform: translateX(100px);

This transform moves our div in the x axis (horizontal axis) by 100 px. We can use negative values here to move it to the left.

transform: translate(-50px, 30px);

This transform moves our div to the left by 50px, then moves it down the y axis by 30px.

We had to give this one a margin-bottom to see this text.

transform: skew(20deg);

This transform acts as if we were pulling it from a corner, it was set to 20 degrees.

transform: skew(10deg, 5deg);

This transform does the same as the previous, but it was pulled from the x and y axis by 10 and 50 degrees respectively.

Combining transform properties.

transform: rotate(10deg) scale(0.7);

This transform rotates our div by 10 degrees, and is also scaled down to 0.7, leaving our box 70% as big as it was originally.

transform: translateX(-200px) rotate(180deg);

This transform moves our div 200px to the left, which is why it is a negative value first, and then rotates our box by 180 degrees, leaving it upside down.

The text is------transform: translateX(-200px) rotate(180deg);
